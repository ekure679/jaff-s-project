About Livestock Farming

Definition of Livestock Farming

Livestock farming, also known as animal husbandry, is the practice of breeding, raising, and caring for domesticated animals for various purposes, including food, fiber, and labor.

Challenges Faced by Livestock Farmers

  • Poor animal health management
  • Inadequate access to veterinary services
  • High feed and production costs
  • Market fluctuations and price instability
  • Environmental issues and climate change

Solutions to the Challenges

  • Implementing effective disease prevention and control measures
  • Improving access to veterinary care and services
  • Exploring alternative and cost-effective feed options
  • Strengthening market linkages and diversifying products
  • Adopting sustainable farming practices and adapting to climate change

Advantages of Livestock Farming

  • Source of nutritious food (meat, milk, eggs)
  • Contribution to rural livelihoods and economies
  • Utilization of marginal lands
  • Diversification of income sources
  • Potential for value-added products and by-products
